General Information:

The Holocaust was known in Nazi Germany as the Final Solution. As part of the Final Solution, the Germans would kill any people who they thought were lesser than them. They mainly targeted Jews, although mentally ill people were killed as well. The Germans tried to separate the Jews from the rest of society and sent them to concentration camps to be tortured in large numbers. Although Germany almost succedded, the Allies won World War II and the surviving Jews went to displaced person camps. (Source: "Introduction to the Holocaust.")

Holocaust Statistics:

  • Approximately 6 million Jews killed
  • Approximately 3.5 million survivors
  • 1.5 million children estimated to have been killed
  • 6 death camps
(Sources: "The History Place - Holocaust Timeline: Statistics of the Holocaust.", "The Holocaust.")
